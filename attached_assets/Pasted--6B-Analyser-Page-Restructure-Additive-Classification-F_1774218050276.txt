#6B - Analyser Page Restructure + Additive Classification Fix for THA Scoring

What & Why
Restructure the dedicated Analyser page into a clear decision-support page and align it with the current THA scoring philosophy.

This pass has two linked goals:

1. Rebuild the Analyser page around a clear comparison structure:
- current product
- cleaner shop option
- whole-food / from-scratch option

2. Update the additive/scoring logic so it reflects an important THA rule:
- herb extracts and natural flavourings must be classified as additives

This is important because the current behaviour is producing misleading equivalence in some products. For example, Heinz Tomato Ketchup was scoring the same as Wilkin & Sons ketchup, even though THA does not consider those products the same quality. We want the scoring and analysis system to reflect that difference more honestly.

Core product principle
The Analyser should help users answer:
- what is this product?
- why did it score like this?
- what is a cleaner packaged option?
- what is the whole-food option?
- what should I do next?

The scoring explanation should also align with the current THA philosophy:
- real, familiar ingredients score better
- industrial additions reduce the score
- users can go further by filtering additives and E-numbers
- at THA, herb extracts and natural flavourings are treated as additives

Done looks like

Analyser page structure
- The dedicated Analyser page is clearly laid out in the following order:
  1. Product header + score + quick verdict
  2. Why this scores the way it does
  3. Current product
  4. Cleaner shop option
  5. Whole-food / from-scratch option
  6. Compare your options
  7. Refine by ingredients
  8. Action area

Scoring philosophy placement
- The full THA score explanation appears near the top of the Analyser page in a “Why this scores the way it does” section
- It is not hidden in a tooltip only
- It should feel like a trust-building explanation

Scoring rule update
- THA now classifies herb extracts and natural flavourings as additives
- This classification is reflected consistently in:
  - score calculation
  - analyser explanations
  - filtering where relevant
- Heinz Tomato Ketchup and Wilkin & Sons ketchup should no longer incorrectly score the same if their ingredient quality differs due to these additive rules

Language consistency
- Use the agreed THA language system
- Prefer terms like:
  - real ingredients
  - industrial ingredients
  - refine by ingredients
  - specific additives
- Avoid overly technical or fragmented language where not needed

Scope safety
- Keep this pass focused on the dedicated Analyser page and directly related scoring/additive classification logic
- Do not redesign unrelated pages
- Do not rebuild the entire rating engine unless needed for this rule update
- Do not drift into homepage/demo/onboarding/auth work

Out of scope
- Full landing page work
- Demo mode work
- Broad basket redesign
- Full ratings system rewrite beyond the needed additive-classification update
- Major backend architecture refactors
- Full CMS/story/about content work
