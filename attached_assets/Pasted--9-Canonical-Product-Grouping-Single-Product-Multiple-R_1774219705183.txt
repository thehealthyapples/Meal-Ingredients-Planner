#9 - Canonical Product Grouping — Single Product, Multiple Retailers

What & Why
Currently, the analyser and product views show duplicate entries for the same product across different supermarkets (e.g. Heinz Tomato Ketchup appears multiple times — once per retailer).

This creates clutter and weakens the product experience.

We need to restructure this so that:
- one product is shown once
- all retailers selling that product are grouped inside it

Core principle
A product should be identified independently of retailer.

Retailers are attributes of the product, not separate products.

Done looks like

- Heinz Tomato Ketchup appears once in the analyser
- it has a single score and analysis
- a "Available at" section lists all retailers and prices
- duplicate product cards for each supermarket no longer appear

Tasks

T001 — Identify how products are currently keyed
Inspect how products are currently identified in:
- product search
- analyser
- basket

Determine:
- what fields differ per retailer
- what fields are identical (name, ingredients, size)

T002 — Define canonical product identity
Group products using a normalized identity based on:
- product name (normalized)
- brand
- size/weight

Example:
"heinz tomato ketchup 570g"

Do not include retailer in this identity.

T003 — Group retailer entries under a single product
For matching products:
- merge into one canonical product
- attach retailer entries as a list:
  - retailer name
  - price
  - URL if available

T004 — Update analyser page UI
Replace duplicate product cards with:

Single product card:
- name
- score
- analysis

Add new section:
"Available at"
- list retailers and prices

T005 — Ensure scoring runs once per product
Ensure:
- score is calculated once per canonical product
- not per retailer

T006 — Maintain correct behaviour for variants
Do NOT merge:
- different sizes (e.g. 342g vs 570g)
- materially different variants

Only merge true identical products.

T007 — Keep implementation scoped
Do not:
- redesign the entire product system
- change external APIs
- introduce heavy new data models

Focus on:
- grouping logic
- analyser display

Acceptance checklist

- duplicate retailer-specific products removed from analyser
- one product shown with multiple retailers
- score and analysis are consistent across retailers
- UI clearly shows where product can be bought
- no regressions in product selection or basket flow

At the end, provide a summary:
- how products were grouped
- what fields were used for matching
- how retailer data is attached
- any limitations or edge cases
